Nguyen H. Tran – Research Overview

Short Description

My research interest is mainly on the design, modelling and analysis of cutting-edge applications in communication networks such as

  • HetNet

  • Cloud-computing Datacenters

  • Smart Grids

  • Wireless Virtualization

  • Internet of Things

Overview

My research interest is developing theoretical integrated methods for a practical design of complex networks. Specifically, my research is motivated via three phases: modelling the complexed networks, analyzing the modelled results, and designing algorithms. First, for modelling and analyzing phases, I combine various analytical tools such as stochastic modelling, queueing theory, optimization theory, and game theory as an integrated approach. Then, for the designing phase, I develop practical, robust and scalable algorithms that can achieve provably efficient performance.

Especially, a wide range of cutting-edge applications of complex networks that I focus on includes wireless networks, cloud-computing data center networks and smart power grid networks. With the exponential increase of data demand and capacity of both wireless cellular and WiFi networks, the emergence of data centers as the network infrastructure for various services, and the next generation of the power grid networks for electricity distribution, I believe that we need a solid theoretical and inter-disciplinary approach to tackle the complex dynamics of the underlying networks for a practical design.
